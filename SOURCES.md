# Sources & References

## Primary Religious Texts

- **The Quran** — Primary Islamic scripture containing extensive references to djinn (particularly Surah 72: Al-Jinn, and numerous other verses throughout)
- **Hadith Collections** — Sayings and traditions of Prophet Muhammad containing references to djinn
  - Sahih al-Bukhari
  - Sahih Muslim
  - Other major hadith collections

## Notes on Sources

### On Popular Culture "Genie" Sources

Western popular culture sources (Disney's *Aladdin*, *I Dream of Jeannie*, etc.) are **not** accurate representations of Islamic theology or authentic Arabian djinn traditions. They represent Orientalist fantasies and Western creative adaptations disconnected from religious and cultural authenticity.

### On Translation Challenges

Arabic religious and theological texts pose translation challenges. Primary Islamic sources should ideally be consulted in original Arabic or with scholarly translations that preserve theological nuance.
